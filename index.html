The four ACT tools — tap any to open full activity

Container · The Sky
The vast, stable space that holds all weather without being changed by it. You are not your thoughts — you are the sky they pass through.
Self-as-context
Orbit · The Distance
Placing thoughts at a distance like objects crossing the sky — they can be watched rather than inhabited. A thought is not a fact.
Cognitive defusion
Anchor · The Now
The sensory reality of the sky returns attention to the present moment — the only moment in which reflection and choice are possible.
Present moment
Compass · The Values
Guiding stars as personal values — fixed points of orientation in a changing world. Navigating by what matters rather than by avoiding discomfort.
Values & action

Stay on the side of growth

Ask yourself these before and during every session. The boundary is not a limitation — it is the work done well.

Pivot

The pivot to the present

When the weather turns heavy — use this
"Thank you for sharing that. It sounds like a heavy cloud. For right now, let's bring our attention back to the sky above us. Let's find the light of [star or object] together and just rest our eyes there for a moment."

After the session, check in privately, express genuine care, and share the support contacts from the Support tab.

Three-step universal script

Every session. Every length. Every context. Know this so well you can leave it behind.

1
Arrive · Ground in the present
The anchor
"Notice your breath and the air on your skin. Let the day fall back a little. We are here now."
2
The cosmic hook · Open perspective
The orbit
"This light left its star five hundred years ago. The star itself may no longer exist. But the light is here, in your eyes, right now, in this moment."
3
The reflection · Invite the observing self
The container
"You are the sky that is holding this moment. What does that feel like?"

Keep in mind

Silence is not empty. It is where the work happens. Hold it without filling it.
Your quality of presence matters more than perfect language. Be where you are.
The sky does the teaching. Your role is to make the looking possible.
You are not here to fix anyone. You are here to hold a space in which they can notice something.
Knowing your limits clearly is not a weakness. It is what makes the space safe.
